# First Days 2026 Schedule — Williams College First-Year Orientation

First Days is Williams College orientation for new students. The program helps participants build connections, acclimate to campus, learn about college resources, and prepare for classes. Junior Advisors guide students through required sessions, with evening options based on interests. Legend: R = Required, E = Encouraged, W = Health & Wellness Focus.

## Thursday, September 3 — Move-In Day
Morning & afternoon: student arrival with pre-scheduled check-in times (8am–12pm); campus tours beginning at 10:30am; family lunch (11am–1pm); parent reception at the President's House Lawn (2:30–4pm); First Entry meetings (1:30–2:15pm, required); VOICES and PARTY SAFER community sessions (2:30–6pm, required).
Evening: Dining Hall Debut with entry groups (6–8pm); "First Night Fest" featuring ice cream, games, a bounce slide, and a mascot meet-and-greet (8–10pm).

## Friday, September 4
Breakfast (7:30–9am); First Class Meeting with the President, deans, and student leaders (10–11am, required); Student Life & Leadership welcome (11–11:30am, required); President's Picnic (11:15am–1pm); EphVenture program check-ins begin (1pm).

## Saturday–Monday, September 5–7 — EphVentures
Multi-day outdoor adventure programs. Sunday includes Catholic Mass (7:30pm). Monday features free time and a hiking debrief (10am–1pm); post-EphVenture entry reunions (1–2pm, required); a "Framing Community" dialogue on belonging (2:15–3pm, required); Power Tools workshops addressing inclusion (3:30–5pm, required); a campus picnic dinner (5:30–8pm); and evening programs including a sustainable sorbet social and an Indigenous history discussion.

## Tuesday, September 8
"Getting to know CSS" orientation (10–10:30am, required); Mind, Body, Spirit wellness session (10:30–11:30am, required); placement exams (12–4pm); health center and chaplaincy open houses; a Hopkins Forest hike with environmental staff (6–7:30pm, registration required); musical bingo with prizes and ice cream sundaes (6–8pm); and a library mini-golf course experience (7–11pm, registration required).

## Wednesday, September 9
"Looking Ahead to the Academic Life" presentation featuring an honor code discussion and writing resources (10–11am, required); Academic Expo in the libraries (11am–12:30pm, required); religious community events (Muslim lunch, Catholic lawn games, Jewish NewBQ); academic advising appointments (1:30–4pm); an Academic Support Fair highlighting tutoring and the writing center (2–3:15pm, required); "Preparing to Write Your First College Paper" (4–5pm, required); and a Night at the Clark Art Institute featuring exhibitions, workshops, performances, a film screening, and stargazing (7–10pm).

## Thursday, September 10 and Beyond
First-Year Student-Athletes affinity spaces event (Sept 10, 6–8pm); Davis Center Block Party with food and performance groups (Sept 15, 5–7:30pm); SEXPO healthy relationships discussion (Sept 16, 6:30–7:30pm); a Jamboree performance showcase (date TBD); and the Purple Key Fair with student organizations (Sept 19, 8–10pm).
