# Williams College Student Handbook — Policy Overview

The student handbook from the Dean of the College sets out the policies governing student life at Williams. It covers academics, residential life and housing, student conduct, alcohol and substance use, health and wellbeing, civic responsibilities, and additional administrative policies.

## Academics
The handbook covers course registration, placement exams (AP, A-Level, IB), the academic calendar and degree requirements, the Honor Code and academic misconduct procedures, grade complaints and appeals, classroom recording restrictions, leaves of absence and readmission processes, and reduced course load tuition policies.

## Residential Life & Housing
Policies address room assignment and selection processes, moving procedures and housing deadlines, guest policies and occupancy rules, prohibited items and fire safety, noise policies, pet restrictions, room decoration and maintenance, personal property liability, and storage and key control.

## Student Conduct
The code of conduct emphasizes community respect. It details disciplinary procedures for social misconduct, Honor Code violations and academic integrity, sexual misconduct reporting and investigation, the fraternity prohibition policy, and interim restrictions and disciplinary records.

## Alcohol & Substance Policy
The handbook establishes that "Williams prohibits the abuse of alcohol" and prohibits illegal drugs. It details responsible-party standards, stating that parties over 20 people require registration and that "hard alcohol and common source alcohol (kegs, punch, etc.) are prohibited." Medical amnesty protections apply when students seek help during substance-related emergencies.

## Health & Wellbeing
Topics include student health insurance requirements, confidentiality policies, and smoking restrictions.

## Civic Responsibilities
The handbook provides jury duty information and exemptions, voter registration procedures, and Selective Service requirements.

## Additional Policies
Other sections cover financial aid and bill payment, athletic equipment loans, religious holiday accommodations, registered student organizations, and campus event planning and posting policies.
