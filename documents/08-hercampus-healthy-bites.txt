# Healthy Bites – College Edition

Craving a late-night snack but want to keep it healthy? Sometimes we don't want to keep going to the dining hall just to order late-night. Other times, all we want is something simple so that we can quickly make it. Here are a few easy, fast, tasty recipes you could try without having to go grocery shopping.

## 1. Easy Yogurt Bites
All you need is a cup of any type of yogurt (I'm obsessed with Skyrs) and fruit. Bonus points if you have nonfat Greek yogurt. Personally, my go-to mix is always strawberry yogurt with blueberries. Add a sweetener if you like — I prefer honey or maple syrup. This is the perfect time to bust out your ice cube trays/molds. Mix all of your ingredients and place them in your ice cube trays! If you choose a larger fruit, cut or mash it into smaller pieces. If you have nut butter, layer it lightly over your yogurt. Now you have a great alternative to ice cream!

## 2. Salad in a Jar
Become BFFs with your campus's salad bar and get to work. Grab some diced or baby tomatoes, cheese of your liking, shredded carrots, quinoa, and chickpeas. I also like the taste of olive oil in my salads, so I'd add some dressing to my jar. The basic idea is that you need your grains, vegetables, and protein. Put it all in your jar and shake it up! Now you have a great salad jar you can eat on the go or store in your refrigerator for later.

## 3. Fruit Wraps
Toast a piece of tortilla. While that's cooking, scavenge for fruits and any sort of nut butter. I strongly recommend slicing your fruits. Layer your tortilla with a light spread of nut butter and honey if you like them extra sweet. Then add your fruits and fold them into the tortilla. You can eat these now or store them for later — it's better to eat when they're hot, so microwave for around 30 seconds if you stored them in the fridge.

It is so tempting to just buy snacks from the vending machines, but it is incredibly important to take care of your body. It's also important that we don't obsess over trying to eat healthy 24/7 since we are living on campus and most of us rely on dining hall food. It may be helpful to substitute certain foods for healthier alternatives, like quinoa instead of white rice, and whole wheat bread for white flour bread. Best of luck with your midterms!
